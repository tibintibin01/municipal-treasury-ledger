# Business Tax & Permit Collection System

Office User Manual and Daily Workflow Guide

Version: v0.3.44

Prepared for: Municipal Treasurer's Office staff

## Read This First

This manual is written for real office use. It is for staff who may be moving from index cards, logbooks, manual ledgers, and Excel sheets into a computerized system for business tax and permit collection.

You do not need to be a computer expert. Read the short workflow first, then use the detailed sections only when you need help with a specific task.

Important: Before deleting records, restoring backups, importing large files, archiving old years, changing settings, or managing users, ask the Treasurer or system Admin.

## 1. What This App Is

The Business Tax & Permit Collection System is a Windows desktop app for recording business registration, renewal assessment, business tax and permit charges, payments, OR numbers, balances, reports, and related audit records.

It is designed for a Municipal Treasurer's Office that wants to move away from manual index card recording and keep business tax and permit collection records in one organized system.

The app helps record:

- Business owner details.
- Business establishment details.
- New registration and renewal records.
- Yearly assessments.
- Capital and gross sales.
- Business tax.
- Mayor's permit.
- Fees, surcharge, and penalty.
- Payments and OR numbers.
- Annual or quarterly payment schedules.
- Paid amount and remaining balance.
- Delinquent accounts.
- Reports, notices, exports, receipts, backups, and audit logs.

## 2. Who Should Use It

This app is intended for:

- Cashiers who record payments.
- Treasury staff who maintain business tax and permit records.
- Treasurers who review balances, collection summaries, and delinquent accounts.
- Admin users who manage system settings, user accounts, backup, restore, and audit review.

## 3. What This App Is Not

This app is focused on business tax and permit collection. It is not a full replacement for every Municipal Treasurer's Office system.

It does not currently replace:

- Real Property Tax systems.
- Market rental systems.
- Other revenue modules outside business tax and permit collection.
- Certified official government receipt systems unless approved by your office and configured under proper policy.
- A networked multi-user database system for many simultaneous workstations.

Note: The receipt preview is useful for taxpayer reference and internal checking. Follow your office policy for official receipt issuance.

## 4. Main Benefits

The app helps the office by:

- Making business records easier to search.
- Reducing repeated writing on index cards.
- Keeping owner, assessment, and payment records connected.
- Showing balances automatically.
- Helping identify delinquent accounts.
- Creating reports for review.
- Exporting records to Excel or CSV.
- Creating backups.
- Showing who changed important records through the audit log.

## 5. The Simple Office Workflow

If you remember only one page, remember this one.

### Start of Day

1. Open the app.
2. Log in using your own account.
3. Check the Dashboard.
4. Confirm the correct data file is shown at the lower-left side of the app.
5. If instructed by the Treasurer, click **Run Check** on the Dashboard to update delinquency status.

### When a Taxpayer Comes In

1. Search the business name, owner name, TIN, address, OR number, or year.
2. Select the correct business record.
3. Check the Owner tab for correct owner and business details.
4. Check the Assessment tab for the correct year and amount due.
5. Open the Payments tab.
6. Enter the payment date, OR number, payment schedule, and amount.
7. Save the payment.
8. Check the paid amount and remaining balance.
9. Preview the receipt if needed.

### End of Day

1. Review recent payments.
2. Print or export needed reports.
3. Click **Backup**.
4. Wait for the backup verification result.
5. Close the app.

Important: A backup is part of the daily routine, not an optional extra.

## 6. Getting Started

### Recommended Computer Setup

Use the app on an office computer with controlled access.

Recommended setup:

- Windows 10 or Windows 11.
- A dedicated folder for the app.
- A secure Windows user account.
- BitLocker or similar disk protection if available.
- A backup folder outside the app folder.
- A second backup location, such as an external drive or network folder.

### Open the App

1. Open the app folder.
2. Double-click `MunicipalTreasuryLedger.exe`.
3. Wait for the login screen.

### First Login

On first run, the app creates a temporary administrator account.

Default first-run account:

- Username: `admin`
- Password: `admin123`

Important: Change this password before entering real office records.

### Log In

1. Type your username.
2. Type your password.
3. Click **Login**.

If login fails several times, your account may be temporarily locked. Ask the Admin for help.

### Log Out

1. Save your work.
2. Close the app window.
3. The next user should open the app and log in with their own account.

The app may also lock after inactivity.

## 19. Backup and Restore

### Why Backup Matters

A backup protects the office from accidental deletion, computer damage, database problems, and ransomware.

### Set Backup Folder

1. Click **Backup To**.
2. Choose the approved backup folder.
3. Click **OK**.

Recommended backup locations:

- External drive.
- Network folder.
- Secure cloud-synced office folder, if allowed by office policy.

Avoid keeping the only backup beside the main database file.

### Create a Backup

1. Click **Backup**.
2. Choose the file location if asked.
3. Enter backup password if creating an encrypted backup.
4. Wait for verification.
5. Confirm that the backup was saved successfully.

### Restore from Backup

Restore replaces the current database with the backup data.

1. Click **Restore**.
2. Select the backup file.
3. Read the warning.
4. Confirm only if approved.
5. Wait for restore to finish.
6. Review the records after restore.

### Restore Last Save

Use **Restore Last** only when you need to go back to the previous saved database.

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Click **Restore Last**.
3. Read the warning.
4. Confirm only if approved.
5. Review the records after restore.

Important: Never restore a backup just to "check what is inside." Ask the Admin to copy the database to a safe test folder if review is needed.

## 26. Troubleshooting

### I Cannot Log In

Check username and password. If still not working, ask an Admin to reset your password.

### My Account Is Locked

Wait for the lockout period or ask the Admin.

### I Cannot See Some Tabs

Your role may not allow access. Ask the Admin or Treasurer.

### A Field Is Highlighted

The app found invalid or missing information.

1. Read the warning message.
2. Check the highlighted field.
3. Correct the value.
4. Save again.

### Import Skipped Rows

Open the import preview notes. Correct the Excel or CSV file, then import again.

Common causes:

- Missing required fields.
- Invalid dates.
- Invalid money values.
- Duplicate OR number.
- Payment amount greater than balance.

### Backup Failed

Check:

- Backup folder exists.
- Disk has enough free space.
- Network drive is connected.
- You have permission to write to the folder.

### Restore Failed

Check:

- Backup file is correct.
- Backup password is correct if encrypted.
- File is not damaged.
- You have permission to restore.

### Dark Mode Did Not Apply

Click **Theme** again or close and reopen the app.

### Dashboard Looks Empty

Check:

- Correct year is selected.
- Business records exist.
- Assessments exist.
- Payments exist.
- Dashboard was refreshed.

## 29. Data Protection Notes

The app includes login, roles, audit log, backup, and protected sensitive fields. Still, good office practice is required.

Recommended production controls:

- Use Windows account restrictions.
- Use BitLocker or similar disk encryption.
- Keep backup files secure.
- Keep backup passwords secure.
- Limit Admin accounts.
- Do not copy the database to personal devices.
- Do not send taxpayer records through personal email or chat.

Important: The current app does not use SQLCipher full-database encryption. Treat the computer, database file, and backups as controlled office records.

## 30. Limitations to Remember

Current limitations:

- Single-workstation desktop use.
- No simultaneous multi-user network editing.
- No Real Property Tax module.
- No SMS or email notification system.
- Receipt preview is not automatically a certified official receipt replacement.
- Full SQLCipher database encryption is not implemented.

These limitations do not make the app useless. They simply define its proper scope: business tax and permit collection ledger work for an office that needs better records, search, reports, backups, and accountability.
